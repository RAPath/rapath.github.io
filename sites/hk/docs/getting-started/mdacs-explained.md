# MDACS — Voluntary System Explained

> *HK-unique · De facto mandatory market access requirement*

## Official Voluntary Status vs Market Reality

MDACS listing is **officially voluntary** — no current Hong Kong law mandates device registration before sale. In practice, listing is essential because:

1. **Public hospital procurement** — DH progressively requires MDACS listing for all applicable medical devices in public tenders
2. **Private healthcare** — most private hospitals independently require MDACS evidence
3. **Market credibility** — an HKMD listing number signals compliance to healthcare buyers

## DH Procurement Phases

| Stage | Date | Requirement |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Stage A | June 2023 | Preference for listed devices in DH tenders |
| Stage B | November 2024 | Listed or application submitted with assigned number |
| **Stage C** | **23 March 2026** | **Mandatory listing for all AMDs in DH procurement** |

## Applicable Medical Devices (AMDs)

- General: **Class II, III, IV**
- IVD: **Class B, C, D**

Class I (general) and Class A (IVD) are not subject to the procurement mandate.

:::warning Stage C becomes effective 23 March 2026.
To remain eligible for DH public procurement from this date forward, all applicable medical devices (Classes II–IV / IVD B–D) must have either: (1) a valid MDACS listing, or (2) a pending application with an assigned reference number submitted before 23 March 2026. Devices without either status will be ineligible for DH tender participation.
:::

Devices with applications submitted before 23 March 2026 but not yet approved may be subject to transition provisions. Contact the MDD for clarification on in-flight applications near the deadline.

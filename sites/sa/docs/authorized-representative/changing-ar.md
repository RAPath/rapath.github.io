# Changing Your Authorized Representative

## Why changing an AR is significant

In Saudi Arabia, the **MDMA certificate is held in the AR's name** — not the manufacturer's. Changing your AR therefore directly affects your device registration status and requires regulatory action.

## The change process

To change your AR:
1. **Appoint the new AR** — the new AR must hold (or obtain) a valid MDEL AR license
2. **Execute a new AR contract** — properly authenticated and legalised
3. **Notify the SFDA** via GHAD — submit a change notification with the new AR's details
4. **Update the MDMA certificate** — the SFDA will issue an updated certificate reflecting the new AR
5. **Update all labelling** — labels, packaging, and IFU must be updated to show the new AR's name and address

## Commercial implications

Until the SFDA processes the AR change and issues an updated MDMA certificate, devices labelled with the old AR's details may face distribution complications. Plan AR changes carefully:

- Allow sufficient time for SFDA processing (weeks to months)
- Coordinate labelling artwork changes in advance
- Ensure the old AR cooperates — they must consent to the transfer and release the MDMA certificate

## Risk of distributor-as-AR

This process illustrates why using a **distributor as AR** creates risk. If you wish to change distributors, the existing distributor (as AR) controls the MDMA certificate. A non-cooperative former distributor can significantly delay market re-entry with new distribution arrangements.

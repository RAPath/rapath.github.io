# Fees & Charges

## Overview

Overview

Health Canada charges non-refundable fees for Device Licence applications, annual Device Licence maintenance, and Medical Device Establishment Licences (MDELs). Fees are established in the Medical Devices Regulations Fees Order and are indexed annually for inflation. Fee amounts vary based on device class and business size, with reductions available for small businesses and non-profit organisations.

## Device Licence application fees

Fees are charged for each Device Licence application submitted to Health Canada. The applicable fee is based on device class:

| Device class | Fee category |
|---|---|
| Class II | Standard fee |
| Class III | Standard fee (higher than Class II) |
| Class IV | Standard fee (higher than Class III) |

The current fee amounts are published on the Health Canada website and in the Medical Devices Regulations Fees Order.

## Annual Device Licence fees

Device Licence holders pay an annual fee to maintain each Device Licence in good standing. Annual fees are invoiced by Health Canada and must be paid to avoid licence suspension or cancellation.

## Medical Device Establishment Licence (MDEL) fees

MDEL holders pay an annual fee based on the activities covered by the MDEL (importing, distributing, manufacturing Class I).

## Fee reductions for small businesses

Health Canada provides fee reductions for small businesses that meet defined criteria:
- Fewer than 50 full-time employees (globally), or
- Annual gross revenue below a defined threshold (see current regulations)

Small businesses may be eligible for a significant reduction (typically 50–75%) in application and annual fees.

## Fee waivers

Fee waivers may be available for:
- Devices intended solely for humanitarian use
- Devices intended solely for export from Canada (not for sale in Canada)
- Government departments and agencies

## Refund policy

Fees are generally non-refundable once a submission has been accepted for review. Partial refunds may be available if a submission is withdrawn before review begins.

## How to pay

Fees are invoiced electronically by Health Canada through the HRES portal. Payment must be made within the specified period or the application may be placed on hold.

> Official resource: [Health Canada — Fees for medical device submissions](https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/drugs-health-products/medical-devices/application-information/fees.html)

Current fee amounts

Specific fee amounts are not provided in this overview because they are subject to periodic adjustment and are established in the Medical Devices Regulations Fees Order (SOR/2024-XX). Manufacturers should consult the current Fees Order or contact Health Canada's Medical Devices Directorate for current application and annual maintenance fee amounts applicable to their device class and business size.

# MDEL Fees & Charges

## Annual MDEL fee

MDEL holders pay an **annual fee** to maintain their establishment licence. The fee covers all activities listed on the MDEL (importing, distributing, manufacturing Class I).

Annual fees are invoiced by Health Canada. Non-payment can result in MDEL suspension.

## Fee structure

The annual MDEL fee is set by regulation. The current fee amounts are published on the Health Canada website.

## Small business fee reduction

Health Canada provides **fee reductions** for small businesses. A small business is eligible for a reduced MDEL fee if it meets the criteria defined in the applicable Fees Order:
- Fewer than **50 full-time employees** globally, **or**
- Annual gross revenue below the threshold set in the Fees Order

Small businesses typically receive a 50% or greater reduction in annual MDEL fees.

## Fee waivers

Fee waivers may be available for:
- Government departments and agencies
- Not-for-profit organisations in specified circumstances

## How fees are paid

Fees are invoiced by Health Canada, typically annually. Payment is made through Health Canada's online payment system.

## Consequences of non-payment

Failure to pay annual MDEL fees within the required period may result in:
- MDEL suspension
- Loss of ability to legally import or distribute devices in Canada

> **Official resource:** [Health Canada — Fees for medical device submissions](https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/drugs-health-products/medical-devices/application-information/fees.html)

MDEL fees must be paid within 30 days of invoice. Health Canada may provide a grace period of up to 60 days before suspension proceedings begin, but reliance on this grace period is not recommended. Applicants should confirm current payment terms with Health Canada's online portal.

# Open Consultations

Health Canada consults publicly before implementing significant regulatory changes. Open consultations are published in the **Canada Gazette, Part I** and on Health Canada's consultation page.

*Always check official sources for the current status of consultations, as they open and close throughout the year.*

---

## How to find open consultations

**Canada Gazette, Part I:**
[canadagazette.gc.ca/rp-pr/p1](https://www.canadagazette.gc.ca/rp-pr/p1/index-eng.html)
Search for entries related to the Food and Drugs Act or Medical Devices Regulations.

**Health Canada — Consultations:**
[canada.ca/en/health-canada/programs/consultation-medical-devices](https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/programs/consultation-medical-devices.html)

**Consulting with Canadians:**
[consult.canada.ca](https://consult.canada.ca)

---

## Recently closed consultations (for reference)

### UDI framework
Health Canada consulted extensively on proposed UDI requirements. Industry feedback focused on alignment with FDA and EU UDI requirements to minimise compliance burden for multi-market manufacturers.

### Post-market amendments
Health Canada consulted on proposed amendments to strengthen post-market surveillance and problem reporting requirements for Class III and IV devices.

---

## How to participate

Any person may submit comments on a Canada Gazette, Part I consultation during the comment period (typically 30–75 days). Comments are submitted electronically through the consultation platform. Industry associations (MEDEC, AdvaMed Canada members) often coordinate industry responses.

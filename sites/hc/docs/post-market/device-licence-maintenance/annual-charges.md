# Annual Charges

## Device Licence annual fees

Manufacturers holding one or more Device Licences pay an **annual fee** for each licence. Health Canada invoices licence holders annually.

Key points:
- Annual fees apply to all **active** Device Licences
- Fees are set by the Medical Devices Regulations Fees Order
- Small businesses may be eligible for reduced fees

## MDEL annual fees

MDEL holders pay a **separate annual fee** for their establishment licence. The MDEL fee covers all activities listed on the licence.

## Payment and consequences

Annual fees must be paid within the period specified on the invoice. Non-payment can result in:
- **Reminder notices** from Health Canada
- **MDEL or Device Licence suspension** if fees remain unpaid after a defined period
- Legal consequences under the *Financial Administration Act*

## Fee management best practice

- Set reminders for annual fee due dates
- Maintain current billing contact information in HRES
- Cancel licences for products no longer sold in Canada to avoid unnecessary fees
- Apply for small business fee reductions when eligible

## Current fee schedule

The current annual fee amounts are published on the Health Canada Medical Devices Directorate website and in the Medical Devices Regulations Fees Order (SOR/98-282). Fees are subject to periodic revision (typically annually, including inflation-based adjustments). Manufacturers should verify current amounts before submitting payment.

> **Official resource:** [Health Canada — Medical Device Fees](https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/drugs-health-products/medical-devices/application-information/fees.html)

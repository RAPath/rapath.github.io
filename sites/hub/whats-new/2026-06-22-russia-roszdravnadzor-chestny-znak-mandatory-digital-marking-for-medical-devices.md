Russia's national mandatory labelling system, **Chestny ZNAK** (Honest Sign), applies to medical devices. All medical devices placed on the Russian market must carry a **Data Matrix** (2D barcode) code issued through the Chestny ZNAK system and operated by the Centre for Research in Perspective Technologies (CRPT). The system enables end-to-end track-and-trace from manufacturer to patient.

Key obligations include:

- **Manufacturers and importers** must register with Chestny ZNAK and obtain unique serialised Data Matrix codes for each unit or consumer pack.
- **Distributors and healthcare institutions** must scan and report product movements at each transfer point, including receipt, sale, and disposal.
- **MDLP (Medical Device Labelling and Product circulation)** is the specific sub-system for medical devices within the broader Chestny ZNAK infrastructure.

📅 **Effective / published:** Phased rollout; mandatory for most medical device categories.

Foreign manufacturers exporting medical devices to Russia must arrange Data Matrix code generation and label application before shipment, or work with a Russian importer who is a registered Chestny ZNAK participant. The Authorised Representative (AR/LOI holder) typically coordinates compliance. Non-compliance — including devices without a valid Data Matrix or unregistered in MDLP — results in seizure and prohibition of sale.

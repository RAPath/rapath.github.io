The National Medicines Regulatory Authority (NMRA) of Sri Lanka regulates medical devices under the NMRA Act No. 5 of 2015 and subsidiary regulations. The NMRA is responsible for the registration, quality assurance, and post-market surveillance of medical devices imported or manufactured in Sri Lanka.

Key requirements:

- **Device Registration**: Medical devices must be registered with the NMRA before importation or sale. The registration dossier includes technical documentation, labelling, and evidence of marketing authorisation in the country of manufacture or a recognised reference market.
- **Risk-based approach**: The NMRA applies a risk-based classification approach broadly aligned with the IMDRF/GHTF framework.
- **Local Authorised Representative**: A Sri Lanka-based entity is required to hold the NMRA registration on behalf of foreign manufacturers.
- **GMP/Quality System evidence**: Manufacturers must provide evidence of GMP compliance, typically a Free Sale Certificate or GMP certificate from the country of origin.

📅 **Effective / published:** NMRA Act — 2015; regulations updated periodically

Sri Lanka's regulatory capacity is developing, and submission timelines can vary. Foreign manufacturers should work with a reputable local regulatory consultant or authorised representative familiar with current NMRA requirements and processing norms. Ensure your device holds valid marketing authorisation in its home market, as this is a key reference document for NMRA review.

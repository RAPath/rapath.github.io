China NMPA Quality Management System Overview: NMPA requires manufacturers to implement and maintain a Quality Management System (QMS) in accordance with MDSAR 2021, NMPA Order No. 53, and YY/T 0287-2017 (equivalent to ISO 13485:2016). The QMS applies to all stages of the device lifecycle: design, production, storage, distribution, and post-market surveillance. Core QMS elements include: (1) Management Responsibility—leadership commitment, quality policy, and organizational structure; (2) Resource Management—personnel training, infrastructure, and supplier management; (3) Product Realization—design controls, production planning, and process controls; (4) Measurement, Analysis and Improvement—inspection, testing, complaint handling, and CAPA. The QMS must be documented in a Quality Manual with supporting procedures, work instructions, and records. NMPA verifies QMS adequacy through GMP inspections, and manufacturers must demonstrate continuous improvement through post-market surveillance and risk assessment. QMS compliance is mandatory for market authorization and post-market maintenance of approval.

Supplier Management Requirements: Manufacturers must establish documented procedures to evaluate and approve all suppliers of materials, components, and services critical to device quality and safety. YY/T 0287-2017 requires: (1) Supplier Evaluation—assess supplier capability, quality system certifications (e.g., ISO 13485, ISO 9001), regulatory compliance history, and technical competence before initial approval; (2) Purchasing Data—clearly specify purchased product requirements including technical specifications, drawings, acceptance criteria, process requirements, and applicable quality/regulatory standards; (3) Supplier Monitoring—conduct periodic audits (on-site audits recommended at least once per contract period for critical suppliers, or documented review of quality metrics) to verify continued compliance; (4) Incoming Acceptance—establish acceptance criteria and inspection/testing procedures for all critical purchased materials and components; (5) Supplier Records—maintain comprehensive records of evaluations, approvals, audit reports, certificates of analysis, and documented corrective actions. NMPA expects evidence of supplier audits, quality agreements defining responsibilities, certificates of analysis or compliance documentation, and prompt resolution of supplier non-conformances. Suppliers must comply with applicable regulations in their jurisdiction and provide documentation supporting full device traceability and batch/lot control.

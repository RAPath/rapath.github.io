Design Control Requirements: NMPA expects documented design control processes aligned with YY/T 0287-2017 (equivalent to ISO 13485:2016). Key elements include: (1) Design Planning—define intended use, user needs, and design inputs; (2) Design Input—specify functional and performance requirements with traceability; (3) Design Output—document design specifications with verification against inputs; (4) Design Review—conduct formal reviews at key phases with documented evidence; (5) Design Verification—confirm design outputs meet design inputs through testing/analysis; (6) Design Validation—confirm final design meets user needs and intended use; (7) Design Changes—control and approve modifications with impact assessment. A complete Design History File (DHF) must be maintained and made available during regulatory inspections.

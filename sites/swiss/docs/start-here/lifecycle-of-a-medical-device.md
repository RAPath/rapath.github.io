# Lifecycle of a Medical Device

## Overview
The Swiss regulatory lifecycle follows a defined sequence from initial product definition to post-market monitoring. Understanding this sequence helps manufacturers and CH REPs plan regulatory activities effectively.

## Stage 1 — Device Definition and Classification
Define the intended purpose; determine MedDO or IVDO scope; classify the device (Class I–III or IVD categories). Classification drives all subsequent requirements. → [How Classification Works](../pre-market/classification/how-classification-works)

## Stage 2 — General Safety and Performance Requirements (GSPRs)
Systematic assessment against MedDO Annex I requirements. Map each GSPR to harmonised standards and document compliance evidence. → [GSPR Overview](../pre-market/gspr/gspr-overview)

## Stage 3 — Technical Documentation
Compile Annex II (general technical doc) and Annex III (PMS documentation). Includes device description, GSPR compliance, risk management, and clinical evaluation. → [Technical Documentation Overview](../pre-market/technical-documentation/tech-doc-overview)

## Stage 4 — Conformity Assessment
Complete the class-appropriate conformity assessment (Annex IV for Class I; NB-assessed routes for Class IIa+). Issue Declaration of Conformity. → [Conformity Assessment Overview](../pre-market/conformity-assessment/conformity-assessment-overview)

## Stage 5 — Swissmedic Registration
Notify or register the device in DAPI before (or within 30 days of) market placement. → [Registration Overview](../pre-market/swissmedic-registration/registration-overview)

## Stage 6 — Market Placement and Labelling
Place the device on the market with compliant labelling (including DE/FR/IT). Apply UDI. → [Labelling Requirements](../post-market/labelling/labelling-overview)

## Stage 7 — Post-Market Surveillance
Active PMS system — monitor complaints, vigilance data, literature, and PMCF data. Update technical documentation as required. → [PMS Overview](../post-market/pms/pms-overview)

## Stage 8 — Vigilance and FSCA
Report serious incidents to Swissmedic via eVigilance. Implement and notify FSCAs. → [Vigilance Reporting](../post-market/vigilance/what-must-be-reported)

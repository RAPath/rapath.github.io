# Advertising & Promotion

## Overview
Advertising and promotion of medical devices in Switzerland is regulated by the **Therapeutic Products Act (TPA) Chapter 4** and the associated **Ordinance on Therapeutic Products Advertising (Arzneimittelwerbeverordnung, AWV, SR 812.212.5)** — the AWV applies primarily to medicinal products but certain provisions extend to medical devices. Swissmedic has authority to assess and challenge non-compliant advertising.

## Key Principles
- All claims made about a medical device must be accurate, verifiable, and consistent with the intended purpose and technical documentation
- Claims must not be misleading or exaggerated
- Advertising must not create an impression of properties that the device does not have
- Advertising must not suggest the device is safer or more effective than available evidence supports

## Professional vs Consumer Advertising

**Professional advertising** (directed at healthcare professionals):
- Generally less restrictive than consumer advertising
- Must include the device name, indications, contraindications, and relevant warnings
- Comparative claims are permitted if based on documented evidence
- Off-label promotion is prohibited — claims must align with the registered intended purpose

**Consumer advertising** (directed at the general public):
- Medical devices intended for self-use by lay persons may be advertised to consumers
- Advertising must not be directed at children or suggest devices are suitable for paediatric use if not registered for that use
- No testimonials from healthcare professionals for consumer advertising that imply medical endorsement beyond what the technical documentation supports
- Prescription-equivalent devices (devices requiring professional involvement) should not be advertised to the general public in a way that encourages self-prescription

## Swissmedic Oversight
Swissmedic can review advertising on its own initiative or following complaints. Enforcement actions range from requesting withdrawal of non-compliant advertising to formal administrative proceedings under TPA.

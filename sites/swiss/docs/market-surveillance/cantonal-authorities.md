# Cantonal Authorities

## Overview
Medical device **market access is a federal matter** (Swissmedic, federal law). However, **healthcare practice** and aspects of healthcare facility oversight are regulated at the **cantonal (state) level**. This creates a layered system where both federal and cantonal authorities have roles relevant to medical devices.

## Cantonal Roles Relevant to Medical Devices

**Healthcare facility licensing**
Cantonal health departments license hospitals, clinics, and medical practices. Facility licensing conditions may indirectly affect how devices are used, stored, and maintained within the facility.

**Professional licensing**
Cantonal authorities license healthcare professionals (physicians, nurses, physiotherapists). Professional licensing conditions may affect which device types can be operated by licensed professionals in that canton.

**Hospital exemption oversight**
While Swissmedic receives the formal notification for the hospital exemption (TPA Art. 9 para. 2(a)), cantonal health authorities may also have a role in overseeing in-house manufactured devices used within healthcare facilities in their jurisdiction.

**Radiation protection**
Under the Radiation Protection Act (SR 814.50), cantonal radiation protection offices (operating under federal delegation) play a role in facility licensing for radiation-emitting medical devices. Facilities using X-ray, nuclear medicine, or radiation therapy equipment require cantonal radiation protection authorisation in addition to MedDO compliance.

## Interaction with Swissmedic
Cantonal authorities can refer market surveillance concerns to Swissmedic. In complex cases (e.g. serious incidents at a cantonal facility), Swissmedic and cantonal health authorities cooperate. Primary medical device enforcement is conducted at the federal level through Swissmedic.

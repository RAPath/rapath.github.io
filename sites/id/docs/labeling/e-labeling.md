# e-Labeling

## What Is e-Labeling?

**e-Labeling** refers to the provision of the Instructions for Use (IFU) in electronic format — typically via a QR code on the device or packaging linking to a web-hosted document — rather than as a printed insert.

---

## Legal Basis: Permenkes No. 11 of 2025

**Permenkes No. 11 of 2025** formally recognises e-labeling as a valid method for providing instructions for medical devices in Indonesia. This aligns Indonesia with international trends and with similar provisions in the EU (REGULATION (EU) 2021/2226 on electronic IFU) and other markets.

---

## When e-Labeling Is Permitted

Permenkes 11/2025 permits e-labeling specifically for **devices intended for professional use in clinical settings** (hospitals, clinics, medical professionals). For such devices:
- The IFU may be provided electronically via a durable QR code on the device or packaging
- The linked URL must be maintained for the lifetime of the device on the Indonesian market
- The electronic IFU must be in Bahasa Indonesia (BI language requirement applies equally to e-labeled IFUs)

---

## When Physical Labels Remain Mandatory

Physical printed labels in Bahasa Indonesia are **still mandatory** for:
- **High-risk devices** (Class C and D) intended for home use or patient self-administration
- **Consumer/patient-facing devices** where immediate access to safety information is essential
- **Emergency situations** — all warnings and safety-critical information must be immediately accessible without internet connectivity

**The "safety priority" rule**: Even where e-labeling is used, any warnings, contraindications, dosing information, or critical safety instructions must be available in a **physical format in Bahasa Indonesia**. The physical label cannot simply redirect to a QR code for safety-critical content.

---

## Practical e-Labeling Compliance Approach

For professional-use devices where e-labeling is appropriate:

1. Place a durable QR code on device/packaging linking to the hosted BI IFU
2. Include on the physical label in Bahasa Indonesia: device name, NIE, manufacturer, LAR contact details, lot/batch number, expiry date, storage conditions, key safety warnings, contraindications (if applicable), and a clear QR code linking to the full electronic IFU
3. Ensure the hosted IFU is maintained and accessible throughout the device's Indonesian market life
4. Include a brief statement directing users to access the full IFU electronically
5. Submit the e-labeling approach (including the QR code target URL and the physical label design) as part of the Regalkes dossier

---

## Ongoing URL Maintenance

The electronic IFU URL must remain active for the entire period the device is on the Indonesian market. If the URL changes, a change notification via Regalkes is required. URL expiry or inaccessibility constitutes a labeling non-compliance.

Quality Assurance for e-Labeling: Before submission, verify that (1) the QR code links correctly and consistently; (2) the hosted IFU loads completely and displays properly on multiple devices (mobile, tablet, desktop); (3) the PDF or web version is readable and printable if needed; (4) Bahasa Indonesia content is complete, accurate, and meets all mandatory labeling requirements; and (5) the URL will remain active and accessible throughout the device's market life in Indonesia. Kemenkes may test the QR code and URL accessibility during the review phase and during market sampling post-approval, and non-functional links constitute a labeling compliance violation.

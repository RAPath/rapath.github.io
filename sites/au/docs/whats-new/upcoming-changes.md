# Upcoming Changes

Planning ahead for regulatory changes is essential for sponsors and manufacturers. This page tracks announced and anticipated changes to Australian medical device regulation.

Always verify upcoming change dates and details on the TGA website before making compliance plans based on this page.

---

## Confirmed — With Known Dates

### 21 March 2026 — Mandatory Healthcare Facility Adverse Event Reporting Begins

**What changes:** Healthcare facilities must mandatorily report adverse events involving high-risk medical devices to the TGA. High-risk devices include Class III, AIMD (now Class III), and IVD Class 4.

**Reporting requirements for facilities:**
- Death or serious deterioration: within **10 calendar days**
- Reports submitted via the **ASDER** system

**Who must prepare now:**
- **Healthcare facilities:** Register for ASDER and establish internal reporting processes before March 2026
- **Sponsors of high-risk devices:** Review your vigilance and customer communication processes — hospitals may report events before you are notified

👉 [Healthcare facility reporting — TGA](https://www.tga.gov.au/resources/guidance/reporting-medical-device-adverse-events-healthcare-facilities)

---

### 1 April 2028 — Expanded Mandatory Reporting

**What changes:** Mandatory reporting by healthcare facilities expands to include:
- Adverse events involving **medium-risk devices** (Class IIa, IIb, IVD Class 2, IVD Class 3)
- **Near-miss events** for high-risk devices

**Who must prepare:** Sponsors of medium-risk devices and healthcare facilities not yet in scope.

---

### 1 April 2030 — Further Expansion

**What changes:** Mandatory reporting further expands to additional event types and device classes. Check TGA guidance as 2030 approaches for specifics.

## Confirmed — Date Pending

### Conformity Assessment Procedure Changes

Following the December 2025 consultation, the TGA is expected to implement changes to Schedule 3 conformity assessment procedures — potentially including the phasing out of Part 5 (Product Quality Assurance). Implementation date to be confirmed.

**Who is affected:** Manufacturers currently relying on Schedule 3 Part 5 procedures as their conformity assessment pathway.

👉 [TGA consultation outcomes](https://consultations.tga.gov.au)

---

## Ongoing — Progressive Rollout

### UDI Implementation

Unique Device Identification (UDI) requirements are being phased in progressively across device classes. Higher-risk classes have already been required to comply. Lower-risk classes will be required to comply on a rolling basis.

**Action:** Check the TGA UDI hub for the current implementation timeline for your specific device class.

👉 [UDI rollout — TGA](https://www.tga.gov.au/products/medical-devices/labelling-and-advertising/unique-device-identification-udi-hub)

---

### EU MDR Transition Impact on Australia

EU MDD certificates are progressively expiring and being replaced by EU MDR certificates. This affects Australian ARTG entries supported by MDD certificates:

- MDD-supported Class III applications are now subject to **mandatory audit**
- EU MDR certificates from notified bodies with current designations receive better treatment
- Sponsors should work with manufacturers to plan MDR transition timelines
- ARTG variation applications may be needed as MDD certificates are replaced

👉 [EU MDR transition guidance — TGA](https://www.tga.gov.au/resources/guidance/eu-mdr-transition-guidance-australian-medical-device-sponsors)

---

## Watch This Space

### Essential Principles Alignment with EU MDR/IVDR

The TGA has signalled interest in aligning the Australian Essential Principles with the General Safety and Performance Requirements (GSPRs) in the EU MDR/IVDR. Any changes would require consultation and legislative amendment. No timeline confirmed.

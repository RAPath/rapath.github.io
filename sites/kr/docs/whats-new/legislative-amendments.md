## How Korean legislative amendments work

In Korea, amendments to the Medical Devices Act and IVD Act:

1. Drafted by MFDS or Ministry of Health and Welfare
2. Published for public comment (typically 40 days)
3. Reviewed by the National Assembly (for Act-level amendments) or Cabinet (for Decrees)
4. Enacted with a specified commencement date (often 6–12 months for major changes)

**MFDS Notifications (고시)** can be amended faster — MFDS can update a notification without National Assembly or Cabinet involvement.

## Where to track amendments

- [law.go.kr](https://www.law.go.kr) — track version history of Acts, Decrees, and Rules
- [MFDS website](https://www.mfds.go.kr) — amendments to MFDS Notifications announced in news section

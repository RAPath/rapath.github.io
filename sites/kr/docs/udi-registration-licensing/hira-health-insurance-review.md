**HIRA (Health Insurance Review and Assessment Service, 건강보험심사평가원)** reviews health insurance claims and assesses the clinical value and cost-effectiveness of healthcare technologies, including medical devices.

## MFDS vs HIRA

| Aspect | MFDS | HIRA |
|---|---|---|
| Role | Safety and effectiveness — legal market access | Reimbursement — NHI coverage |
| Requirement | Mandatory for ALL medical devices | Required only for NHI reimbursement |
| Data focus | Safety, technical, clinical evidence | Clinical effectiveness, cost-effectiveness |

## HIRA assessment process

1. Submit coverage application to HIRA after MFDS 품목허가
2. HIRA reviews clinical evidence for the device's clinical effectiveness
3. HIRA evaluates cost-effectiveness (often requiring health economic analysis)
4. NHIS sets the reimbursement price based on HIRA recommendation

## New Technology Assessment (신의료기술평가)

Truly novel devices not fitting an existing reimbursement category must go through HIRA's **New Technology Assessment** before NHI listing is possible. This adds typically 1–2 years to the market access timeline.

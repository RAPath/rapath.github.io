Design controls are a critical chapter of KGMP (aligned with ISO 13485:2016 Section 7.3) and are particularly scrutinised during MFDS inspections for higher-risk devices (Grade III and IV). Deficiencies in design control documentation are among the most common inspection findings.

## Key requirements

- **Design and development planning** — plan describing stages, responsibilities, and timelines
- **Design inputs** — functional, performance, safety, and regulatory requirements documented
- **Design outputs** — documented and verified against inputs before release
- **Design review** — formal reviews at defined stages with documented results
- **Design verification** — confirming design outputs meet inputs
- **Design validation** — confirming the finished device meets user needs and intended use
- **Design transfer** — ensuring design can be manufactured consistently
- **Design changes** — controlled and evaluated for impact before implementation

## MFDS inspection focus

Inspectors will look for:
- Traceability from design inputs through to outputs, verification, and validation
- Evidence that verification and validation were conducted on representative final devices
- Change control records for any design modifications

Production and process controls ensure devices are manufactured consistently and meet specifications (ISO 13485:2016 Section 7.5).

## Key requirements

- **Documented production procedures** — work instructions at all critical steps
- **Process validation** — for processes where output cannot be fully verified by subsequent inspection (e.g., sterilisation, welding, adhesive bonding)
- **In-process inspection and testing** — at defined checkpoints during manufacturing
- **Device identification and traceability** — each device traceable to its components and manufacturing records
- **Contamination control** — particularly for sterile devices and implantables
- **Cleanliness of product** — including particulate and bioburden control where relevant
- **Device history records (DHR)** — a complete manufacturing record for each batch or unit

## Process validation (critical)

Processes that cannot be fully verified by inspection must be **validated** before production begins and re-validated after any significant change.

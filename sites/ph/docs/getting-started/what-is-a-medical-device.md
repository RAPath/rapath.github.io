# What Is a Medical Device?

> *RA 9711 · DOH AO 2018-002 · FDA Philippines / CDRRHR*

## Statutory Definition

Under Philippines law (RA 9711 and DOH Administrative Order No. 2018-002), a **medical device** is any instrument, apparatus, implement, machine, appliance, implant, reagent for in vitro use, software, material, or other similar article — whether used alone or in combination — that is:

- **Intended by the manufacturer** for use in or on human beings
- For the purpose of **diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, treatment or alleviation** of disease
- For **diagnosis, monitoring, treatment, alleviation or compensation** for an injury or handicap
- For **investigation, replacement or modification** of anatomy or a physiological process
- For **control of conception**

The principal intended action must not be achieved by pharmacological, immunological, or metabolic means. A device may be assisted in its function by pharmacological, immunological, or metabolic means without being reclassified as a pharmaceutical product, provided the principal intended action remains mechanical or physical.

## What Is an IVD? — PH-Unique Current Status

An **in vitro diagnostic (IVD) medical device** is a device — including reagents, calibrators, kits, instruments — intended for examination of specimens from the human body.

### IVD Registration — Currently NOT Required — PH-Unique

A critically important distinction in the Philippines: as of the last review (May 2026), **IVD medical devices are currently NOT required to undergo CMDN/CMDR registration with CDRRHR**. This means IVDs may currently be imported and commercially distributed without prior CDRRHR registration.

**However:**
- This status is expected to change — the Philippines has announced plans to introduce IVD registration requirements
- An **LTO (License to Operate)** is still required for entities importing or distributing IVDs
- Monitor the **CDRRHR website and FDA Circulars** for the announcement and timeline of IVD registration implementation

See [What's New](/whats-new/ivd-registration-update) for the latest on IVD registration status.

## What Is Not a Medical Device?

The following are generally excluded:
- **Pharmaceutical drugs** — principal action pharmacological, immunological, or metabolic
- **Cosmetics** — aesthetic purposes without medical claims
- **Food supplements** — nutritional products without medical device claims
- **General consumer goods** — no medical intended purpose

## Borderline and Combination Products

Products at the device-drug boundary are assessed based on principal mechanism of action. Contact the CDRRHR for a formal classification inquiry before proceeding. See [Classification Overview](/classification/overview).

:::tip LTO required even for IVDs
Even though IVDs do not currently require CMDN/CMDR registration, all entities handling IVDs (importers, distributors, manufacturers) must hold a valid **License to Operate (LTO)**. See [Establishment License & LTO](/cdrrhr-operations/establishment-license).
:::

Last reviewed: May 2026. For current information, always verify with CDRRHR at www.fda.gov.ph — regulatory requirements may change.

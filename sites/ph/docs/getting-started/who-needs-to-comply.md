# Who Needs to Comply?

## Overview

All entities in the Philippines medical device supply chain must hold a **License to Operate (LTO)** from CDRRHR. For Class A devices, a CMDN (Certificate of Medical Device Notification) is required. For Class B/C/D devices, a CMDR (Certificate of Medical Device Registration) is required.

## Local Manufacturers

Philippines-based manufacturers of Class A, B, C, and D devices must:
- Hold an LTO as a manufacturer
- Obtain CMDN (Class A) or CMDR (Class B/C/D) before market placement
- Comply with GMP requirements
- Maintain post-market obligations

## Importers

All commercial importers of medical devices must:
- Hold an LTO as an importer
- Import only devices with valid CMDN or CMDR (non-IVD devices)
- Ensure devices comply with Philippine labelling requirements
- Maintain distribution records

## Distributors, Traders and Wholesalers

Entities that distribute, trade, or wholesale medical devices must hold an LTO. The specific LTO category depends on the activity (wholesale, retail, etc.).

## Re-packers

Entities that repackage registered medical devices into smaller units must hold an LTO as a re-packer. The re-packed device must retain its CMDN/CMDR validity.

## Local Representative / License Holder — PH-Unique

For foreign-manufactured devices, the CDRRHR requires a **local license holder** — a Philippines-registered entity that holds the CMDN or CMDR:

| Obligation | Local Representative / License Holder |
|-----------|--------------------------------------|
| Holds CMDN/CMDR certificate | ✅ Yes |
| Submits registration application | ✅ Yes |
| Provides Letter of Authorization from manufacturer | ✅ Yes (notarised declaration required) |
| Post-market obligations | ✅ Yes |
| LTO as importer | ✅ Yes (or separate importer entity) |

## Lifecycle of a Device in the Philippines

| Stage | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | Classify device (ASEAN AMDD Class A/B/C/D) |
| 2 | Confirm not an IVD (currently exempt) or check current IVD status |
| 3 | Obtain LTO (manufacturer / importer / distributor as applicable) |
| 4 | Prepare CMDN application (Class A) or CMDR application (Class B/C/D) |
| 5 | Submit to CDRRHR |
| 6 | Address deficiencies within 90 calendar days if raised |
| 7 | CMDN / CMDR certificate issued |
| 8 | Commercial distribution |
| 9 | Post-market: vigilance, eReport system, maintenance |
| 10 | Certificate renewal before expiry |

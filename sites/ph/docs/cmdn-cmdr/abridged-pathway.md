# Abridged Pathway — ASEAN NRA Reliance

> *PH-Unique · FDA Circular 2022-008 · 30 working-day turnaround*

## Overview

**FDA Circular 2022-008** established the **Abridged CMDR Pathway** — a faster registration route for Class B, C, and D devices that already hold approval from a recognised ASEAN National Regulatory Authority (NRA).

This pathway leverages the technical assessment already conducted by the reference ASEAN NRA, enabling CDRRHR to issue a CMDR within **30 working days of payment receipt** rather than the standard CMDR processing timeline.

## Eligible Reference NRAs

The abridged pathway applies to devices **approved by an ASEAN NRA** that is recognised by CDRRHR for this purpose. ASEAN NRAs include:

| Country | Regulatory Authority |
|---------|---------------------|
| Singapore | HSA (Health Sciences Authority) |
| Malaysia | MDA (Medical Device Authority) |
| Thailand | Thai FDA / MDCD |
| Indonesia | BPOM |
| Vietnam | DAV / Ministry of Health |
| Myanmar | FDA Myanmar |
| Brunei, Cambodia, Laos | Respective national authorities |

Confirm the current list of recognised reference ASEAN NRAs with CDRRHR — the list may be updated.

## Eligibility Requirements

To use the abridged pathway:

1. Device holds a **current, valid approval** from a recognised ASEAN NRA
2. The device submitted to CDRRHR is **technically identical** to the reference-approved device:
   - Same intended purpose
   - Same materials and specifications
   - Same design
3. The applicant declares identical specifications in writing
4. Payment of applicable fees must be received before the 30-working-day clock starts

## 30 Working-Day Timeline

The **30 working-day** turnaround starts from **payment receipt** (not from submission of the application). Ensure fees are paid promptly after the application is accepted as complete.

## Conditions for Withholding Abridged Approval

CDRRHR reserves the right to **withhold or escalate beyond 30 working days** if:
- Safety signals or concerns exist regarding the device
- The reference NRA's review is deemed insufficient for Philippines context
- The device specifications are found to differ from those approved by the reference NRA
- Additional information is required for public health protection

## Abridged Pathway Documentation

The abridged application uses a reduced dossier compared to the standard CMDR:

| Document | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| Reference NRA approval certificate | Current, valid — identifies device |
| Identical specifications declaration | Written declaration from Philippines license holder |
| Abbreviated device description | Consistent with reference submission |
| Letter of Authorization (with notarised declaration) | Required |
| Philippines-compliant labelling | Per AO 2018-0002 |
| LTO | Valid for applicant entity |
| Fee payment | Triggers the 30-working-day clock |

## Strategic Value

If your device holds HSA (Singapore), Malaysian MDA, Thai FDA, or another ASEAN NRA approval, the abridged pathway is the **recommended first-choice approach** for Philippines registration. The 30-working-day timeline is dramatically faster than standard CMDR processing.

**Example:** A device approved by Singapore HSA (one of the fastest ASEAN markets) can leverage that approval to obtain Philippines CMDR in approximately 6 working weeks after payment.

Note: The 30 working-day timeline counts working days only and excludes weekends and Philippine national holidays. Clarify with CDRRHR whether the timeline is extended if deficiencies are raised during the 30-working-day period or whether the clock restarts upon receiving deficiency responses.

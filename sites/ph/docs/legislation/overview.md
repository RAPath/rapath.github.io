# Legislation Overview

> *RA 9711 · DOH AOs · FDA Circulars · ASEAN AMDD*

## Primary Laws

### RA 9711 — Food and Drug Administration Act of 2009

The foundational modern legislation for the Philippines FDA:
- Establishes FDA Philippines and its organizational structure
- Grants FDA Philippines authority over medical devices
- Provides for licensing (LTO) and registration (CMDN/CMDR)
- Sets penalties for violations — including criminal provisions for falsification of certificates
- Provides for Motion for Reconsideration and appeal process
- Protects confidentiality of technical data submitted for registration
- Grants FDA authority to issue emergency authorisations

### RA 3720 — Food, Drugs, Devices and Cosmetics Act (1963)

The legacy legislation with the original definition of "drug" and "device." Still partially applicable — particularly for definitional provisions where RA 9711 cross-references.

### RA 9502 — Universally Accessible Cheaper Medicines Act

Affects pricing provisions for health products. Relevant for manufacturers concerned about price regulation in the Philippines.

## ASEAN Instruments

| Instrument | Role |
|-----------|------|
| **ASEAN Medical Device Directive (AMDD)** | The harmonised framework underpinning Philippines' Class A–D classification |
| **ASEAN Common Submission Dossier Template (CSDT)** | Required format for Class B/C/D (CMDR) applications |
| **ASEAN CSDT guidance documents** | Section-by-section guidance on CSDT preparation |

## How to Access Philippines Legislation

- **FDA Philippines website**: [www.fda.gov.ph](https://www.fda.gov.ph/) — Administrative Orders, FDA Circulars, forms
- **Official Gazette**: Official publication of Philippines Republic Acts and Presidential Decrees
- **Chan Robles Virtual Law Library**: Comprehensive repository of Philippines laws
- **CDRRHR**: [www.fda.gov.ph/center-for-device-regulation-radiation-health-and-research/](https://www.fda.gov.ph/center-for-device-regulation-radiation-health-and-research/) — device-specific guidance

:::info Always check for superseded AOs
DOH Administrative Orders may be revised or replaced. Always confirm you are referencing the current version before preparing an application.
:::

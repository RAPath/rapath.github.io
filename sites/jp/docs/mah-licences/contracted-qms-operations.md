# Contracted QMS Operations

## What can be outsourced under MO 169

Under MO 169, many manufacturing and quality activities can be outsourced to third parties — known as "contract manufacturers" or "outsourced process providers." Activities commonly outsourced include:
- Manufacturing operations (component fabrication, assembly)
- Sterilisation
- Testing laboratories (biocompatibility, electrical safety, etc.)
- Packaging and labelling operations
- QMS documentation services

## MAH's oversight obligations

When the MAH contracts manufacturing or quality activities to third parties, the MAH does **not** transfer its regulatory responsibility. The MAH remains responsible for the quality of all contracted operations and must:

1. **Evaluate and qualify** contracted parties before engaging them
2. **Define responsibilities** in a written contract or quality agreement
3. **Conduct periodic audits** of contracted parties to verify continued compliance with MO 169 and the contract terms
4. **Maintain records** of audit findings and actions taken

PMDA will review the MAH's supplier qualification and oversight records during QMS inspections.

## Sterilisation as a contracted process

If sterilisation is outsourced to a contract steriliser, special attention is required:
- The contract steriliser must hold the appropriate manufacturing registration
- The sterilisation process validation must cover the specific device(s) being processed
- Change notification requirements must be covered in the contract (changes to sterilisation cycle parameters may require Ichibu Henko)

# PMA Process & Timelines

## PMA review milestones

| Step | Timeframe |
|------|-----------|
| Filing review (accept or refuse to file) | 45 days from receipt |
| Substantive review begins | After filing acceptance |
| Major deficiency letter (if needed) | During review |
| Advisory panel (if convened) | Per panel schedule |
| MDUFA goal — Original PMA | 180 calendar days |
| MDUFA goal — Panel Track Supplement | 180 calendar days |
| MDUFA goal — 180-day Supplement | 180 calendar days |
| MDUFA goal — Real-Time Supplement | 90 calendar days |

## Pre-submission strategy

Given the 180-day timeline and high cost of a PMA, pre-submission interaction with FDA is critical:

- **Early Q-submission** — discuss overall PMA strategy, clinical study design, endpoints
- **IDE application** — must be approved before beginning pivotal clinical trial
- **Pre-PMA meeting** — review draft PMA structure and anticipated content
- **Pre-approval inspection (PAI)** — FDA inspects manufacturing facility before approval

## Post-approval conditions

FDA approval of a PMA typically includes **conditions of approval**:
- Post-approval studies (PAS)
- MDR reporting
- Annual reporting
- Device tracking (for certain implants)
- Patient labelling

Failure to comply with conditions of approval may result in withdrawal of approval.

# Lifecycle of a Medical Device in the USA

The regulatory lifecycle of a medical device under FDA encompasses three main phases: pre-market, market entry, and post-market.

---

## Phase 1 — Pre-market

### Step 1: Determine regulatory status
- Is your product a medical device under FD&C Act §201(h)?
- Does CDRH or CBER have jurisdiction?
- Is it a combination product requiring an RFD?

### Step 2: Classify your device
- Search the [FDA Product Classification Database](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/cdrh/cfdocs/cfPCD/classification.cfm)
- Identify the applicable 21 CFR Part and product code
- Determine the device class (I, II, or III)

### Step 3: Determine the market pathway
- **Class I** (most) → General controls only; many are 510(k)-exempt
- **Class II** (most) → 510(k) clearance
- **Class I/II (novel)** → De Novo classification request
- **Class III** → Premarket Approval (PMA)

### Step 4: Establish your Quality System
- Implement 21 CFR Part 820 (QMSR — Quality Management System Regulation) / ISO 13485-aligned QMS
- Establish design controls, device history records, risk management

### Step 5: Gather clinical / technical evidence
- Bench testing, biocompatibility, software validation
- Clinical data (IDE study in USA or acceptance of foreign data)

### Step 6: Submit to FDA
- 510(k), De Novo request, or PMA
- Use eSTAR electronic submission format
- Respond to FDA additional information (AI) requests

---

## Phase 2 — Market entry

### Step 7: Establish registration and device listing
- Register your establishment annually at [FURLS/CDRH portal](https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/cdrh/cfdocs/cfrl/rl.cfm)
- List all devices you manufacture or import for US commerce

### Step 8: Comply with UDI requirements
- Obtain a Device Identifier (DI) from an FDA-accredited issuing agency
- Label your device with the UDI
- Submit device information to GUDID (Global UDI Database)

### Step 9: Ensure labelling compliance
- Adequate directions for use (21 CFR Part 801)
- Prescription or OTC designation
- Required warnings and precautions

---

## Phase 3 — Post-market

### Step 10: MDR obligations
- Monitor complaints and adverse events
- Report deaths and serious injuries within required timeframes
- Submit MDRs via eMDR system

### Step 11: Recalls and corrections
- Report corrections and removals under 21 CFR Part 806
- Conduct recalls in accordance with FDA expectations
- Cooperate with FDA-initiated recall actions

### Step 12: Maintain quality system
- Ongoing CAPA, internal audits, management reviews
- Respond to FDA inspections and 483 observations

### Step 13: Post-market surveillance
- Comply with any §522 post-market surveillance study orders
- Maintain distribution records

### Step 14: Manage changes
- Evaluate design and labelling changes against 510(k)/PMA filing thresholds
- Submit supplements or new 510(k)s when required

Post-market surveillance requirements vary by device class and risk profile. FDA may impose mandatory §522 post-market surveillance study orders for higher-risk devices, particularly those approved under PMA. Class I and II devices may be subject to surveillance only in specific cases.

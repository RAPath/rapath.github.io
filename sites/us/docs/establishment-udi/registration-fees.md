# Registration Fees & MDUFA Payment

## Fee authority

Annual establishment registration fees are authorised by the **Medical Device User Fee Amendments (MDUFA)**. Fees are set annually by FDA and are published in the Federal Register each October for the following fiscal year (October 1 – September 30).

## FY2026 fee (approximate)

| Fee type | Amount (FY2026) |
|---|---|
| Standard establishment registration | ~$7,653 |
| Small business establishment registration | ~$1,913 |

Exact fees are published by FDA annually — check the [FDA user fee website](https://www.fda.gov/industry/fda-user-fee-programs/medical-device-user-fee-amendments-mdufa/device-registration-and-listing-fees) for the current year's amounts.

## Small business qualification

A **small business** for MDUFA registration fee purposes is an entity with annual gross receipts or sales ≤ $100 million (including affiliates). Small businesses may qualify for a reduced registration fee.

## Payment process

Registration fees are paid through FDA's **FEE** system (Federal eCopy Electronic Fee) during the FURLS registration/renewal process. Payment is required to complete registration.

**Consequences of non-payment**

Failure to pay the registration fee by the deadline (typically December 31) will result in:
- Automatic deactivation of your establishment registration
- Prohibition from importing or distributing medical devices in the USA
- Inability to submit premarket applications or device listings
- Requirement to re-register and pay fees to restore active status

Note: Deactivated establishments may still be subject to FDA inspections and enforcement actions.

# FDA Inspection Types & Authority

| Inspection type | Trigger | Key focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Surveillance** | Routine, periodic | QMSR compliance |
| **Pre-approval (PAI)** | Before PMA approval | Data integrity, manufacturing |
| **For-cause** | MDR trends, complaints | Specific issue |
| **Follow-up** | Previous 483 observations | Corrective actions |
| **Unannounced** | FDA discretion | Overall compliance |

See [FDA Inspections & Warning Letters](/docs/qms/fda-inspections) in the Quality System section for full detail.

FDA Inspection Authority

FDA's authority to inspect device manufacturers derives from FD&C Act § 704. FDA may inspect any establishment that manufactures, processes, packs, or holds a device. Inspections may occur without advance notice. Manufacturers must permit FDA inspectors access to all records, facilities, and equipment relevant to device compliance. Refusal to permit inspection is itself a violation.

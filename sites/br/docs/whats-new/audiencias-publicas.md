# Open Audiências Públicas (Public Hearings)

:::info BR-unique
**Audiências Públicas** (public hearings) are a mandatory step in ANVISA's regulatory process for significant new regulations. They allow stakeholders to present oral comments and questions to ANVISA's technical team and collegiate board — more interactive than a written consultation alone.
:::

## Current open audiências públicas

Check ANVISA's website for currently scheduled audiências públicas:
- [ANVISA — Audiências Públicas](https://www.gov.br/anvisa/pt-br/assuntos/regulamentacao/audiencias-publicas)

## Participating effectively

**Preparation:**
- Register early — spaces for oral presentations are limited.
- Review the draft regulation thoroughly before the hearing.
- Prepare clear, concise oral comments (typically 5–10 minutes per participant).
- Focus on specific articles or technical issues, not general positions.

**After the hearing:**
- ANVISA publishes transcripts or summaries of audiências públicas.
- ANVISA's response to comments is published as part of the final regulation's explanatory note (*nota de rodapé* or *análise de impacto regulatório*).

Industry associations (ABIMO, SBCTA) often coordinate collective responses to audiências públicas — consider engaging through your industry association.

# What is a Medical Device?

## The legal definition

Under **RDC 751/2022** and **Lei 6.360/1976**, a medical device (*produto para saúde*) in Brazil is defined as a product — instrument, apparatus, equipment, implant, reagent, software, material or similar article — intended by the manufacturer to be used, alone or in combination, in humans for one or more of the following purposes:

- Diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, prediction, prognosis, treatment or alleviation of disease;
- Diagnosis, monitoring, treatment, alleviation of or compensation for injury or disability;
- Investigation, replacement or modification of the anatomy or of a physiological or pathological process or state;
- Providing information by means of *in vitro* examination of specimens derived from the human body; and
- Control or support of conception.

The product must **not** achieve its principal intended action by pharmacological, immunological or metabolic means — if it does, it is classified as a drug (*medicamento*), not a device.

---

## Intended purpose — the key principle

As in other major regulatory frameworks (EU MDR, FDA), Brazilian law places the **intended purpose** (*finalidade prevista*) at the centre of the definition. The intended purpose is determined by the manufacturer's labelling, instructions for use, and promotional materials.

:::info BR-unique
ANVISA's intended purpose analysis is closely aligned with the IMDRF and EU MDR approach. Unlike the US FDA's "intended use" doctrine, Brazilian law does not distinguish labelling-based and promotion-based intent as separate legal categories — both are considered together.
:::

## Common examples by category

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Diagnostic imaging | Ultrasound, X-ray equipment, MRI systems |
| In vitro diagnostics (IVDs) | Blood glucose meters, pregnancy tests, COVID-19 antigen tests |
| Therapeutic devices | Pacemakers, infusion pumps, ventilators, defibrillators |
| Surgical instruments | Scalpels, electrosurgical units, laparoscopic instruments |
| Implants | Hip replacements, stents, cochlear implants, breast implants |
| Software (SaMD) | AI-powered diagnostic software, clinical decision support |
| Wound care | Wound dressings, surgical drapes (with medical claims) |
| Dental devices | Dental implants, orthodontic brackets, dental chairs |

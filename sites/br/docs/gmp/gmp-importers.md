# GMP for Importers — Verifying Foreign Manufacturer Compliance

Brazilian importers who hold medical device registrations in their own name are responsible for ensuring that the **foreign manufacturer** whose devices they import complies with GMP requirements.

## How importers verify foreign manufacturer GMP compliance

1. Obtain the foreign manufacturer's current **ISO 13485 certificate** or equivalent GMP certification (e.g., EU notified body certificate, FDA establishment registration and QMSR compliance).
2. Include the GMP certificate in the Registro/Cadastro dossier submitted to ANVISA.
3. Conduct periodic supplier audits of the foreign manufacturer — this is a requirement of RDC 752/2022's purchasing controls section.
4. Keep the GMP certificate on file and update it when renewed.

ANVISA may conduct inspections at foreign manufacturer sites for Class IV devices or where there are specific concerns about compliance. ANVISA has bilateral agreements with some foreign regulatory authorities (e.g., FDA, EU competent authorities) for information sharing on GMP inspection outcomes.

Importers are also responsible for implementing post-market surveillance procedures, including collection and evaluation of adverse event reports, and must report serious adverse events to ANVISA within the required timeframe. Importers must maintain records of all complaints and adverse events associated with imported devices.

Importers must establish and maintain written quality agreements with their foreign manufacturers that define responsibilities for GMP compliance, quality system requirements, notification of changes, and provision of audit information. These agreements must be documented and available for ANVISA inspection.

# Multa (Fines) — Calculation & Appeal

## Fine calculation

Fines (*multas*) for health surveillance infractions are calculated under **Lei 6.437/1977** based on:

- **Severity of the infraction** — minor, serious, very serious, or grave;
- **Company size** — small companies and microenterprises receive reduced fines;
- **Previous violations** — repeat violations lead to higher fines;
- **Degree of harm** — actual harm to patients increases the fine; and
- **Cooperation** — cooperation with ANVISA investigators may reduce fines.

### Fine ranges (indicative)

| Infraction severity | Fine range (BRL) |
|---|---|
| Minor infraction | BRL 2,000 – BRL 75,000 |
| Serious infraction | BRL 75,000 – BRL 450,000 |
| Very serious infraction | BRL 450,000 – BRL 1,500,000 |
| Grave infraction (causing serious harm) | Up to BRL 1,500,000+ |

Fine values are updated periodically by ANVISA.

## Appeal process

All fines can be appealed through ANVISA's administrative appeals process:

1. **Preliminary defence** (*defesa prévia*) — within 10 working days of the infraction notice;
2. **First administrative appeal** — within 10 working days of the first decision;
3. **Second administrative appeal** (to ANVISA's collegiate board) — final administrative instance.

After exhausting administrative appeals, judicial review is available in the Brazilian federal courts.

# How to Read ANVISA RDCs & Portarias

:::info BR-unique
ANVISA's binding regulations are issued as **RDCs** (*Resoluções da Diretoria Colegiada*) — resolutions of the Collegiate Board. Understanding how to navigate these documents is essential for regulatory professionals working in Brazil.
:::

## Structure of an RDC

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| Preamble | Legal basis for the regulation (references to Lei 6.360, Lei 9.782, etc.) |
| WHEREAS clauses (*Considerandos*) | Justification for the regulation — regulatory rationale |
| Articles (*Artigos*) | The substantive regulatory requirements |
| Annexes (*Anexos*) | Technical annexes (classification rules, essential requirements, forms) |
| Transitional provisions | How the regulation applies to devices already registered under previous regulations |

## Finding and accessing RDCs

- **Diário Oficial da União (DOU):** All RDCs are officially published in the DOU at [in.gov.br](https://www.in.gov.br).
- **ANVISA legislation portal:** Searchable index of all current ANVISA regulations at [gov.br/anvisa](https://www.gov.br/anvisa/pt-br/assuntos/regulamentacao).
- **LegisWeb:** Third-party database with consolidated versions of ANVISA regulations (subscription).

RDCs may be revoked (*revogada*) or amended (*alterada*) by subsequent RDCs. Always check the current consolidated version rather than reading an original publication that may have been amended.

:::note Official sources
Verify all information against official [ANVISA](https://www.gov.br/anvisa) sources before making regulatory decisions.
:::

RDCs and international harmonisation: Many ANVISA RDCs transpose MERCOSUL GMC Resolutions into Brazilian national law. Additionally, RDC 751/2022 and its companions align with IMDRF guidance and EU MDR principles. When reading RDCs, check the preamble for references to MERCOSUL or international standards to understand the regulatory intent.

# RDC 753/2022 — Adverse Event Reporting & Recalls

RDC 753/2022 — Post-Market Safety & Tecnovigilância

RDC 753/2022 is the primary ANVISA regulation governing post-market surveillance (Tecnovigilância), adverse event reporting, technical complaint handling, and safety-related recalls for medical devices in Brazil. It establishes mandatory adverse event and technical complaint reporting, recall procedures, and post-market surveillance plan requirements.

- Adverse event (evento adverso) reporting obligations;
- Technical complaint (queixa técnica) reporting obligations;
- Reporting timeframes (7-day for deaths/serious unexpected injuries; 30-day for other serious events);
- NOTIVISA as the mandatory reporting platform;
- Annual adverse event summary report requirements;
- Recall (recolhimento) procedures — voluntary and compulsory;
- Field safety corrective actions (FSCAs) and field safety notices; and
- ANVISA's powers to order compulsory recalls and device interdictions.

- [NOTIVISA](https://notivisa.anvisa.gov.br)

**Reporting obligations by actor**

RDC 753/2022 establishes mandatory reporting obligations for:
- Manufacturers and importers (registration holders) — must report all adverse events and technical complaints
- Distributors — must report when they become aware of adverse events or technical complaints
- Healthcare facilities — must report serious adverse events and technical complaints to NOTIVISA
- Healthcare professionals — voluntary reporting is accepted via healthcare facility channels

Post-market Surveillance Plans (PMS Plans)
Registration holders must develop and maintain a post-market surveillance plan proportionate to device risk classification and intended use. The PMS plan must include:

- Surveillance objectives and methodology;
- Adverse event and technical complaint monitoring strategy;
- Data sources and collection frequency;
- Analysis procedures and trend identification;
- Communication protocols with ANVISA and healthcare facilities; and
- Plan review and update frequency (typically annually or when significant changes occur).

PMS plans must be submitted with the registration application or during the annual report cycle.

# RDC 751/2022 — Core Medical Device Registration Regulation

**RDC 751/2022** (*Resolução da Diretoria Colegiada*) is the **primary regulation** for medical device registration in Brazil. It replaced the former RDC 185/2001 and fundamentally modernised Brazil's medical device regulatory framework, aligning it more closely with international standards (IMDRF, EU MDR, ISO).

## Key provisions

| Topic | Key content |
|---|---|
| Scope | Defines which products are medical devices and subject to this regulation |
| Classification | Four risk classes (I–IV); rules in Annex I |
| Registration pathways | Notificação, Cadastro, Registro — by class |
| Technical dossier | Essential Requirements (Annex II) and documentation requirements |
| Clinical evidence | Requirements for clinical evaluation data by class |
| Registration holder | Duties of the registration holder (manufacturer or importer) |
| Post-market | PMS plan requirements; link to RDC 753/2022 for adverse events |
| Changes | Post-registration change management (Petição de Alteração) |
| Renewal | 10-year registration validity; renewal process |

## The RDC 751/2022 package

Regulatory package: RDC 751/2022 was issued on 26 May 2022 together with four companion RDCs forming a comprehensive modernised regulatory framework:
- **RDC 752/2022** — GMP (CBPF)
- **RDC 753/2022** — Adverse event reporting and recalls
- **RDC 754/2022** — Labelling
- **RDC 755/2022** — Establishment authorisation (AFE)

Essential Requirements in RDC 751/2022 Annex II are aligned with IMDRF guidance and established international standards (ISO 13485, ISO 14971, IEC 60601 series, ISO 10993, IEC 62304, and IEC 62366). Manufacturers may demonstrate compliance with Essential Requirements through: (1) recognised ABNT standards (which adapt ISO/IEC equivalents); (2) equivalent ISO/IEC standards directly; or (3) device-specific technical justification approved by ANVISA. The most common pathway for international manufacturers is use of ABNT equivalents or ISO/IEC standards listed in the ANVISA harmonised standards catalogue.

Add after 'Technical dossier' row in the table: 'Essential Requirements in RDC 751/2022 Annex II are aligned with IMDRF guidance and established international standards (ISO 13485, ISO 14971, IEC 60601 series, ISO 10993, IEC 62304, and IEC 62366). Compliance with recognised ABNT standards (adaptations of ISO/IEC equivalents) is accepted as means of demonstrating compliance with these Essential Requirements.'

Registration validity and renewal: Medical device registrations under RDC 751/2022 are valid for 10 years from the date of approval. Renewal applications must be submitted at least 90 days before expiration to avoid service interruption. Renewal requires an updated post-market surveillance summary and confirmation of ongoing GMP compliance (AFE currency).

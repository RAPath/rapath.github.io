# Authorized Representative (RUS REP)

## What is a RUS REP?

A **Russian Authorized Representative (RUS REP)** is a legal entity or individual based in Russia who is formally appointed by a foreign manufacturer to act as their representative before Roszdravnadzor. For foreign manufacturers, appointing a RUS REP is **mandatory** — foreign companies cannot submit registration applications directly.

## Who needs a RUS REP

Any foreign manufacturer who wishes to register a medical device for the Russian market must appoint a RUS REP before submitting a registration application. The RUS REP must be appointed before the dossier submission, as the Power of Attorney to the RUS REP is a required document.

## What the RUS REP does

The RUS REP acts as the legal point of contact between the foreign manufacturer and Roszdravnadzor. Their responsibilities include:

- **Submitting the registration application** on behalf of the manufacturer
- **Corresponding with Roszdravnadzor** during the review process and responding to requests for additional information
- **Post-market obligations** — filing adverse event reports, import reports, and FSCA notifications on behalf of the manufacturer
- **Maintaining the Registration Certificate** — notifying changes, managing amendments, and handling re-registration
- **GRLS maintenance** — ensuring the GRLS entry remains accurate

## Appointing a RUS REP

To appoint a RUS REP, the foreign manufacturer must issue a **Power of Attorney** authorising the RUS REP to act on their behalf. For documents issued outside Russia, the Power of Attorney must carry an **Apostille stamp** in accordance with the Hague Convention.

## RUS REP liability

The RUS REP shares joint legal responsibility with the manufacturer for compliance with Russian medical device regulations. This shared liability extends to timely adverse event reporting, import reporting, and post-market surveillance obligations. Choosing a qualified, experienced, and financially stable RUS REP is critical, as the manufacturer remains ultimately accountable for regulatory non-compliance even when the RUS REP acts on their behalf.

## EAEU pathway

The RUS REP requirement also applies under the EAEU pathway when Russia is the reference state for the registration application. For EAEU registrations with a non-Russian reference state, the specific representative requirements of the relevant member state apply.

:::info Official sources
Government Decree No. 1684: [government.ru](https://government.ru/)
:::

RUS REP requirements under EAEU: When registering under the EAEU pathway with Russia as the reference state, the RUS REP (or equivalent authorized representative) must still be appointed and maintains the same core responsibilities: submitting the dossier, corresponding with the EEC, and handling post-market obligations. However, some administrative procedures may differ under EEC Decision No. 46 compared to the national pathway. Manufacturers should verify specific requirements with their RUS REP when transitioning to EAEU registration.

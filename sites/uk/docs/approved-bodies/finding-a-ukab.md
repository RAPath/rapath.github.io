## Overview

How to identify the right UK Approved Body for your device — using MHRA's designated list, understanding scope of designation, and selecting a UKAB for your device type.


## MHRA's designated UKAB list

MHRA maintains a current list of designated UK Approved Bodies at:
[gov.uk/government/publications/approved-bodies-for-medical-devices](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/approved-bodies-for-medical-devices)

The list shows each UKAB's:
- Name and contact details
- Scope of designation — device types and conformity assessment procedures for which they are designated
- UKAB identification number (required on UKCA-marked devices assessed by that UKAB)

## Scope of designation

Each UKAB is designated for specific **device types** and specific **conformity assessment procedures**. A UKAB may be designated to:
- Audit QMS for active implantable devices but not for IVDs
- Conduct type examinations for Class IIa devices but not for Class III
- Assess List A IVDs but not Class III general devices

**Match your device type and required conformity assessment procedure to the UKAB's scope.** A UKAB cannot assess a device outside its designated scope.

## Current UK Approved Bodies

Key UKABs designated by MHRA include:
- **BSI Group (British Standards Institution)** — broad scope covering general devices, IVDs, AIMDs
- **SGS UK Ltd** — scope covering various device types
- **Other designated bodies** — check the current MHRA list as designations change

## Selection considerations

| Factor | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Scope match | The UKAB must be designated for your device type and procedure |
| Capacity | UKABs have limited capacity — early engagement is essential, especially for 2030 UKCA deadline |
| Experience | Choose a UKAB with demonstrated experience in your specific device category |
| Timelines | Request indicative timelines at initial enquiry — these vary significantly |
| Cost | Obtain fee estimates as part of initial engagement |

# Dpco

This page covers related Indian legislation applicable to medical devices.

The **New Drugs and Clinical Trials Rules 2019 (ND&CT Rules)** govern clinical investigation approvals from CDSCO. The **Drugs (Prices Control) Order 2013 (DPCO)** administered by NPPA regulates prices of certain medical devices, including coronary stents, knee implants, and other notified devices.

> **Official source:** [CDSCO — Acts & Rules](https://cdsco.gov.in/opencms/opencms/en/Acts-Rules/)

As of the latest NPPA notifications, regulated medical devices under DPCO include coronary stents, knee implants (artificial joints), cardiac implants, intraocular lenses, and other devices notified by the government. The list is periodically updated through NPPA office memoranda available on the NPPA website.

NPPA determines and notifies price ceiling caps for regulated devices, which manufacturers and importers must comply with. Price revisions are typically conducted at specified intervals (e.g., annually or bi-annually) and published through NPPA office memoranda. Prices must not exceed the notified ceiling price, and any deviation requires explicit approval.

# Telemedicine

> **Official sources:** [CDSCO](https://cdsco.gov.in); [ABDM — NHA](https://abdm.gov.in); [IMDRF](https://www.imdrf.org)

## Overview

Telemedicine in India is regulated separately under the Telemedicine Practice Guidelines issued by the Medical Council of India (now National Medical Commission). Medical devices integrated into telemedicine platforms must comply with both telemedicine and device regulations.

## Regulatory Framework

Telemedicine regulation is governed by:
- Telemedicine Practice Guidelines (National Medical Commission)
- Information Technology (Intermediaries Guidelines) Rules 2011
- Data Protection and Privacy frameworks

## Device Integration in Telemedicine

When medical devices are used in telemedicine contexts:
- Device must be separately approved as medical device by CDSCO
- Telemedicine platform must comply with data security and privacy requirements
- Remote monitoring devices must ensure data integrity and encryption
- Platform must maintain audit trails and ensure interoperability with ABDM if applicable

## Key Compliance Areas

- **Patient consent**: Informed consent for remote consultation and data sharing
- **Data security**: Encryption, secure transmission, and HIPAA/ISO 27001 compliance
- **Licensure**: Healthcare professionals providing telemedicine must be licensed in India
- **Medical record retention**: Digital records must be retained per regulatory requirements
- **Emergency protocols**: Procedures for escalation to in-person care when required

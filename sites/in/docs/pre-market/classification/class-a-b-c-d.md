# Class A · B · C · D — Overview

## Class A — Low risk

**Characteristics:** Non-invasive; minimal or no direct contact with patient; lowest potential harm.

**Examples:** Tongue depressors, examination gloves, non-sterile wound dressings, walking frames, hospital furniture.

**Licensing:**
- Import: Form MD-15 (registration / self-declaration)
- Manufacture: State Licensing Authority (SLA)

**GMP:** Schedule 5 Part I (basic GMP requirements).

---

## Class B — Low-to-medium risk

**Characteristics:** Invasive (short-term or non-surgical), or active therapeutic devices with low risk.

**Examples:** Hypodermic needles, blood pressure monitors, infusion sets, hearing aids, breathing tubes.

**Licensing:**
- Import: Form MD-14 via CDSCO / SUGAM
- Manufacture: State Licensing Authority (SLA)

**GMP:** Schedule 5 Part I.

---

## Class C — Medium-to-high risk

**Characteristics:** Invasive for long-term use, implantable (non-cardiac), or active devices with significant energy delivery.

**Examples:** Spinal implants, vascular grafts, haemodialysis machines, long-term urinary catheters, ventilators.

**Licensing:**
- Import: Form MD-14 via CDSCO / SUGAM
- Manufacture: CDSCO

**GMP:** Schedule 5 Part I and additional requirements for the device type.

---

## Class D — High risk

**Characteristics:** Implantable cardiac/CNS devices, devices in direct contact with the central cardiovascular system, or life-sustaining devices.

**Examples:** Cardiac pacemakers, implantable defibrillators, drug-eluting coronary stents, neurostimulators, HIV diagnostic kits (IVD Class D).

**Licensing:**
- Import: Form MD-14 via CDSCO / SUGAM
- Manufacture: CDSCO

**GMP:** Schedule 5 Part I and Part II (full quality system requirements comparable to ISO 13485).

**Note**: Hearing aids classification may vary; some advanced hearing aids with signal processing features may be reclassified as Class C. Verify specific device classification with CDSCO before submission.

# Show Cause Notices

CDSCO enforcement actions are taken under the **Drugs and Cosmetics Act 1940** and the Medical Devices Rules 2017. Appeals against CDSCO orders are made to the Central Licensing Authority or, ultimately, to the Courts.

> **Official source:** D&C Act 1940, Sections 22–27B; MDR 2017, Chapter VIII

Show Cause Notices Overview

Show Cause Notices Overview

**What is a show-cause notice?**
A show-cause notice is a formal CDSCO directive requiring a manufacturer, importer, or distributor to explain why enforcement action (suspension, cancellation, or seizure) should not be taken against them.

**Grounds for issuance:**
Show-cause notices are issued when:
- GMP inspection reveals significant non-conformities
- Adverse events or safety issues are reported
- Quality or testing failures are identified
- Licensing conditions are violated
- Complaint investigations confirm misconduct

**Response requirements:**
- Manufacturer must respond within the specified timeframe (typically 10–15 days from receipt)
- Response must address each observation in detail
- Supporting documentation and evidence of corrective action required
- Late or inadequate responses may result in immediate enforcement action

**Possible outcomes:**
1. Closure if response satisfactorily addresses all observations
2. Follow-up inspection to verify corrective action
3. Escalation to suspension or cancellation of license
4. Seizure of non-compliant devices

Show Cause Notices Overview

A show-cause notice is an enforcement tool issued by CDSCO when non-compliance is identified. The notice requires the manufacturer, importer, or distributor to explain why their license should not be suspended or cancelled.

Key procedural points:

- Issued following inspection or complaint-based investigation
- Recipient typically has 10–15 days to respond with evidence and corrective actions
- Response must address each non-conformity cited
- CDSCO reviews the response and decides whether to:
  - Accept corrective actions and close the case
  - Proceed to license suspension or cancellation
  - Issue additional enforcement action

Failure to respond or inadequate response strengthens CDSCO's case for license suspension or cancellation.

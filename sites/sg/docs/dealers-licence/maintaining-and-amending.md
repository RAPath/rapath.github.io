# Maintaining and Amending a Licence

## Obligation to notify HSA of changes

Licence holders must notify HSA when certain details covered by the dealer's licence change. Changes that typically require a licence amendment include:

- Changes to the **device risk class** scope of the licence (e.g. expanding from Class B to Class C devices);
- Changes to **QMS certification** status (e.g. a new or updated ISO 13485 certificate);
- Changes to **site details** (warehouse address, premises, storage conditions);
- Changes to the **Person-in-Charge (PIC)** or other key personnel named on the licence;
- Changes to the **company name or structure** (e.g. following a merger or acquisition).

Licence amendments are submitted through the SHARE portal:

[hsa.gov.sg/medical-devices/dealers-licence/amend-licence](https://www.hsa.gov.sg/medical-devices/dealers-licence/amend-licence)

## Ongoing QMS surveillance

After the initial licence is granted, the QMS is subject to ongoing surveillance by HSA-recognised auditors. Companies should ensure their QMS remains current and that surveillance audits are conducted as required.

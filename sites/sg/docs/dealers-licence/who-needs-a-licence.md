# Who Needs a Dealer's Licence?

## The trigger — regulated dealing

A dealer's licence is required before engaging in any of the following regulated activities in Singapore:

| Activity | Licence needed |
|---|---|
| **Manufacturing** medical devices in Singapore | Manufacturer's licence |
| **Importing** medical devices into Singapore for supply | Importer's licence |
| **Wholesaling** medical devices (selling to another business) in Singapore | Wholesaler's licence |

Retail sale directly to the end user (e.g. a pharmacy selling devices to patients) is **not** a dealer's licence activity under the HP(MD) Regulations — but the pharmacy must ensure they only supply registered devices.

## Who does NOT need a dealer's licence

- **Overseas manufacturers** supplying devices to a Singapore importer — the Singapore importer holds the licence, not the overseas manufacturer.
- **Healthcare institutions** (hospitals, clinics) that use medical devices on patients — they need to ensure devices are registered, but do not hold a dealer's licence for device use.
- **Retailers** selling registered Class A or other registered devices directly to consumers — retail supply is not covered by the dealer's licence requirement (though they may have other obligations under the HPA).
- **Logistics and freight providers** who merely transport devices without ownership or supply activity.

## Multiple licence types

A single company often performs multiple activities and may need more than one licence type. Common combinations:

- A Singapore subsidiary of an overseas manufacturer that **imports** devices and also **wholesales** them to hospital distributors needs both an **importer's** and a **wholesaler's** licence.
- A contract manufacturer in Singapore making devices for export typically needs a **manufacturer's** licence.
- A trading company that imports devices for its own hospital group's use needs an **importer's** licence; if it then on-sells to other hospitals, it also needs a **wholesaler's** licence.

## The HSA requirements tool

Use HSA's interactive tool to determine the licence type(s) needed for a specific activity:

[hsa.gov.sg/medical-devices/registration/requirements](https://www.hsa.gov.sg/medical-devices/registration/requirements)

- [HP(MD) Regulations 2010 — Part 4](https://sso.agc.gov.sg/SL/HPA2007-S436-2010)

Note: While an overseas manufacturer does not need a dealer's licence in Singapore, they must designate or appoint a Singapore-based local agent or representative to support regulatory communications and liaison with HSA. This is a mandatory requirement under the HP(MD) Regulations 2010, Part 4. The designated agent acts as the point of contact for all regulatory matters and must be authorised to represent the manufacturer.

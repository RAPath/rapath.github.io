# Singapore in the International Regulatory Landscape

## Singapore as a regulatory hub

Singapore occupies a distinctive position in the global medical device regulatory landscape. It is simultaneously:

- A founding member and active participant of the **International Medical Device Regulators Forum (IMDRF)**;
- A leading voice in **ASEAN medical device harmonisation** through the ASEAN Medical Device Committee (AMDC);
- A jurisdiction that formally recognises approvals from the world's five major reference agencies (TGA, EU NB, Health Canada, MHLW, FDA) as the basis for streamlined registration.

This combination makes Singapore both a strategically important market in its own right and a **gateway to the broader Southeast Asian region**.

## Why Singapore matters for market access strategy

For overseas manufacturers, Singapore's regulatory reliance framework means that existing approvals from major reference jurisdictions — particularly combinations of CE marking + TGA, or FDA + Health Canada — can significantly accelerate Singapore registration via the Abridged, Expedited, or Immediate routes.

Singapore registration, in turn, is recognised or accepted as part of national regulatory submissions in multiple ASEAN countries, and a Singapore Free Sale Certificate is commonly required for ASEAN market entry applications.

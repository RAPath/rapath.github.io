# Advertisements and Promotions

## Legal framework

The advertising and promotion of medical devices in Singapore is governed by the [Health Products Act (Cap. 122D)](../../legislation/health-products-act.md) and HSA's guidelines on advertisements and promotions for medical devices.

It is the responsibility of the **advertiser** to ensure all advertising and promotional materials comply with applicable legislation and guidelines.

## Key principles

- **Only registered devices may be advertised** for supply in Singapore. Advertising an unregistered device as available for supply is a regulatory non-compliance.
- **Claims must match the registered intended purpose.** Promotional claims must not go beyond the intended purpose approved at registration.
- **Claims must be accurate, balanced, and not misleading.** Comparative claims (claiming superiority over competitor products) must be substantiated.
- **Off-label claims are prohibited.** Advertising a device for a use not covered by its registration is an offence.
- **Testimonials and endorsements** must be genuine, relevant, and not misleading.

## HSA's advertising guidelines

HSA has published detailed guidelines on the advertising and promotion of medical devices, covering permitted and prohibited claims, advertising to healthcare professionals, and digital/social media advertising:

[hsa.gov.sg/medical-devices/advertisements-promotions](https://www.hsa.gov.sg/medical-devices/advertisements-promotions)

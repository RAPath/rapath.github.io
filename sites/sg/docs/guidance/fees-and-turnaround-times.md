# Fees and Turnaround Times

## Where to find current fees and TATs

Fees and turnaround times (TATs) for medical device registration and dealer's licence applications are published on the HSA website and updated periodically:

[hsa.gov.sg/medical-devices/fees](https://www.hsa.gov.sg/medical-devices/fees)

Always refer to this page for current rates before preparing a budget or submission timeline. Do not rely on figures cited in older guidance documents or third-party sources.

## Factors affecting fees

Fees typically vary by:
- Device class (A, B, C, D)
- Evaluation route (Full, Abridged, Expedited, Immediate)
- Grouping type (single device vs grouped submission)
- Application type (new registration, change notification, licence application)

## Turnaround time and Input Requests

Published TATs are measured from the date HSA confirms receipt of a complete, screenable application. Applications that fail initial screening are returned without being clocked (the TAT timer does not start). Input Requests (IRs) issued by HSA during evaluation pause the clock — the time spent waiting for the applicant's response is not counted toward the published TAT. The clock restarts once HSA receives the applicant's response.

Responding to Input Requests (IRs) promptly and completely is critical to achieving published turnaround times. Delays in providing requested information can significantly extend the total evaluation timeline, as the clock restarts only after HSA receives a complete response.
